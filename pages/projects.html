Sequential jump detection at high frequency via e-values and online FDR control

TER 2025–2026, supervised by Alain Celisse (SAMM)

Problem
Detect price jumps online in high-frequency data under an Itô semimartingale, while controlling the false discovery rate sequentially, a setting where Benjamini–Hochberg becomes invalid because PRDS fails under dense jumps.
Method
GROW e-values built as integrated likelihood ratios under a Gaussian prior, using a causal MedRV spot-volatility window; fed into five e-value FDR procedures (e-BH, e-LOND, e-LORD, e-SAFFRON, stopped e-BH) and benchmarked against BH on Lee–Mykland and BNS p-values. Monte-Carlo grid over Heston + Merton/Hawkes regimes, three frequencies, M = 100 replications.
Result
BH violates FDR at ∆t = 5 s (observed 0.096 vs. threshold 0.094 at α = 0.05), while all e-value procedures control FDR at every frequency. Stopped e-BH gives the best power–safety trade-off (power 0.349 vs. BH 0.441, FDR ≤ 0.016). Identified and quantified the alpha-death failure mode of e-LORD/e-SAFFRON when ω₁ is mis-scaled.

2D Ising model — Monte Carlo simulation

Problem
Simulate the phase transition of a 2D ferromagnet and recover the critical temperature.
Method
Metropolis–Hastings algorithm on a square lattice; magnetisation and energy measured as a function of temperature; finite-size scaling to extrapolate the Curie temperature.
Result
Recovered Tc ≈ 2.269 J/kB (consistent with Onsager's exact result); observed critical slowing-down near Tc.

Photovoltaic power forecasting

2022–2023 · TIPE (14,5/20)

Problem
Forecast the power output of a photovoltaic panel from meteorological features.
Method
Physical modelling of panel output from solar irradiance and meteorological data; Monte Carlo simulation to propagate uncertainty over the panel's parameters.
Result
Empirical distribution of power output under varying irradiance conditions; sensitivity analysis identifying the most influential physical parameters.

Statistical sports betting — Kelly criterion

2024 · TIPE (14/20)

Problem
Can a bettor achieve positive long-run returns by exploiting mispriced bookmaker odds?
Method
Expected-value analysis on historical bookmaker markets; bankroll dynamics under the Kelly criterion; variance-constrained fractional Kelly; drawdown simulation via Monte Carlo.
Result
Identified conditions under which edge is extractable; showed that volatility drag makes full Kelly dangerous even at positive EV. Fractional Kelly materially improves risk-adjusted growth.
